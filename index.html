Top 5 Picks of The Month

Every month, we hand picked the BEST 5 spots for you to go explore along with a map.

1. Tompkin Square Park

Where: Avenue A & E 7th St, New York, NY 10014

The park is one of the best dog parks in Manhattan. It's absolutely the best place to take a walk on a beautiful sunny day. On thr weekends, the park is full of musicians, skaters, dogs and just people! The park is located at the heart of East Village with endless cafes, restaurants, thriftshops and bars around. You can get there by taking either the F(2nd Avenue)or L(1st Avenue) trains.

2. Ludlow Coffee Supplies

Where: 176 Ludlow St, New York, NY 10002

This cozy yet hip cafe always has the best playlist on whenever you walk in. Looking for somewehre to do work? Ludlow provides free unlimited wifi but try to get there early, it gets filled up pretty quick. You can get there by taking the F(2nd Avenue or Delancey Street) train.

3. CornerStone

Where: 17 Avenue B, New York, NY 10009

Also located in East Village, this cafe/restaurant provides quality food within reasonable price. Two of its most famous dishes are the $4 Penne Vodka and $4 two eggs any style with sala, toasts and homefries. Yes that's right, this place will fill you up with just $4! Tell all your college friends who are struggling to save money on food!

4. The Press Lounge

Where: Kimpton Ink48 Hotel, 653 11th Ave, New York, NY 10036

The lounge has one of the best night views in Manhattan. Although the view is beautiful, the price is not. The average price of cocktails there is about $18. But on the bright side, there's no cover fee! So grab your best friends or strangers(kidding), get ready for a night of spring breeze and view of the city that never sleeps. The lounge is located in the Upper West side, you can get there by taking A C E trains(50th Street).

5. The Blind Barber

Where: 339 E 10th St, New York, NY 10009

And we are back in East Village! This daytime barbershop turns into a speakeasy at night! How cool lit is that?! The dance floor is always popping and the music varies from throw back hip-hop to Billboard top 100. If you're trying to find a place to dance the night away, Blind Barber is definitely the place for you to go. Just remember to get there early, line usually starts forming around 12am. But hey, no cover fee! You can get there by taking the L (1st Avenue)train.
